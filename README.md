# 📉 Customer Churn Analysis & Retention Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn for a telecommunications provider. By evaluating demographic data, contract types, service subscriptions, and account charges across 7,043 customers, the dashboard highlights key factors contributing to customer attrition and identifies strategies to improve retention.

## Tools & Technologies Used
- **Power BI Desktop:** Data modeling, DAX measures, dynamic visuals, and interactive slicers
- **Power Query:** Data cleaning, type transformations, and conditional columns
- **Microsoft Excel:** Source dataset

---

## Key Metrics & Insights

### 1. Overall Churn Highlights
- **Total Customers:** 7,043
- **Overall Churn Rate:** 26.54% (1,869 churned customers)
- **Total Revenue Impact:** Substantial revenue loss driven primarily by short-term contract cancellations.

### 2. High-Risk Customer Segments
- **Contract Type:** Customers on **Month-to-Month contracts** experienced the highest churn rate compared to those on 1-year or 2-year agreements.
- **Payment Method:** Highest churn occurred among customers paying via **Electronic Check**.
- **Tenure:** Customers within their first 12 months of service showed the highest risk of leaving.
- **Tech Support & Security:** Customers without Tech Support or Online Security add-ons churned at significantly higher rates.

## Strategic Recommendations
1. **Incentivize Long-Term Contracts:** Offer small discounts or bundled perks to encourage Month-to-Month customers to transition to 1-year or 2-year plans.
2. **Onboarding Support:** Focus retention efforts on new customers during their first 6–12 months through proactive check-ins and onboarding assistance.
3. **Promote Value-Added Services:** Encourage adoption of Tech Support and Online Security services, as subscribers to these features exhibit much higher retention rates.
